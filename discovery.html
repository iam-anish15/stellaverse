Apollo 11

1969: Neil Armstrong & Buzz Aldrin walk on the Moon, humanity's giant leap beyond Earth.

Voyager 1

1977 launch, now the farthest human‑made object, carrying Earth’s message into interstellar space.

Mars Perseverance

2021: Searching for ancient life on Mars and caching samples for future return missions.

Saturn’s Rings

Shattered moons of ice and rock form these iconic, ever‑evolving rings around the gas giant.

Jupiter’s Storm

A 300+‑year tempest, the Great Red Spot reveals the dynamic weather of the largest planet.

Venus's Rains acid

Venus has sulfuric acid rain, but it never hits the ground. Due to the extreme surface heat (over 460°C), the acid evaporates mid-fall.

Milky Way

Our home: a spiral of 100 billion stars, full of mysteries across 100 000 light‑years.

Andromeda

Nearest spiral galaxy, slowly drifting toward us for a collision in ~4 billion years.

NGC 1052-DF2 (The Ghost Galaxy)

Almost no dark matter was detected in this galaxy (2018).

Sagittarius A*

The supermassive hole anchoring our galaxy, with the mass of 4 million suns in a tiny volume.

Black Holes Can Produce Sound

NASA detected sound waves from a black hole in the Perseus galaxy cluster.

Wandering Black Holes

Black holes aren't always stationary — they can roam around space!

Hubble

Since 1990, capturing breathtaking deep‑space images and reshaping our cosmic perspective.

James Webb

Launched 2021, peering into the universe’s infancy, unveiling galaxies and stellar nurseries.

Spitzer Space Telescope

Launched 2003,helped in discovering exoplanets and mapping dust clouds.
